TN Legacy Biointegrity Historical Sites
Sages Ravine, Salisbury CT

The Biological Condition Gradient (BCG) is a conceptual model that describes changes in aquatic communities and provides a more refined way of assigning stream health than the traditional pass/fail approach. The additional fine tuning allows us to identify minimally stressed healthy streams with sensitive taxa found only under least disturbed conditions (e.g. BCG tiers 1 and 2). Macroinvertebrate BCG Metrics were calculated for data collected by CT DEEP from 1989 - 2019.

Diatoms are another diverse biological community that provide a good indication of nutrient conditions in the stream. Portapova & Charles (2007) identified benthic diatoms in rivers and streams that were tolerant of high total nitrogen (TN) conditions. The percent of tolerant TN diatom was calculated for diatom samples collected by CT DEEP from 2002 - 2019.

The map identifies the locations and drainage basins of macroinvertebrate and diatom sites that were observed to 'under perform' for either the BCG or TN Tolerant diatom metric for one or more samples given the high percentage of core forest in the drainage basin. Additional information and the data included in the map can be found https://github.com/marybecker/liss_legacyTN_biointegrity and https://marybecker.github.io/liss_legacyTN_biointegrity/
